# DigiWin Brand & Visual Style Specification

## About DigiWin

DigiWin (Chinese: 鼎捷数智, traditional: 鼎新數智) is an enterprise software company founded in 1982 in Taiwan. The company provides smart manufacturing solutions — ERP, MES (Manufacturing Execution System), WMS (Warehouse Management System), and AIoT (Artificial Intelligence of Things) — to factories across Asia. DigiWin is publicly listed on the Taiwan Stock Exchange (TWSE: 300378) and has over 50,000 factory clients worldwide, with more than 100 customers in Thailand. Foxconn's industrial IoT subsidiary (FII) is a strategic investor. The company's brand slogan is "Realize imagination with digital intelligence."

DigiWin Thailand (digiwin.co.th) is the Thai subsidiary, targeting two audiences: Thai factory operators who need manufacturing software, and ERP distributors/consultants who want to become DigiWin channel partners. The Thailand website uses English as the primary language with a full Thai-language mirror site.

## Logo

The DigiWin logo has two parts: the D mark and the wordmark. The D mark is built from a circle (symbolizing the data world) combined with stylized letterforms from "Digi," forming an abstract letter D with an interior negative space that resembles an eye or vortex. The D mark is rendered in a vertical gradient from Smart Blue (#00AFF0) at the top to Royal Blue (#003CC8) at the bottom.

The English wordmark reads "Digiwin" set in Noto Sans Bold. An optional heritage tagline "Since 1982" appears below the wordmark in a lighter weight. For Chinese-language contexts, the wordmark is 鼎捷数智 (simplified, for mainland China) or 鼎新數智 (traditional, for Taiwan).

The minimum clear space around the logo equals the height of the logo on all sides. In co-branding layouts, DigiWin always appears on the left side, separated from the partner logo by a vertical rule. The logo must never be stretched, rotated, recolored, given drop shadows, or have its proportions altered. On busy or photographic backgrounds, the white monochrome version of the logo is used.

## Color Palette

### Primary Brand Color: Smart Blue (智慧蓝)
The signature DigiWin color is Smart Blue, hex #00AFF0 (RGB 0, 175, 240; Pantone 306 C; CMYK 75/0/5/0). It is a vivid, saturated cyan-blue — noticeably brighter and more energetic than conventional corporate blues. Smart Blue communicates innovation, technological clarity, and forward momentum.

### Primary Auxiliary Colors
These three colors form the core palette alongside Smart Blue:
- Dark Navy, hex #000864 (Pantone 2758 C): A near-black, very deep navy blue. Used for hero section backgrounds, dark page sections, headings on light backgrounds, and dramatic contrast with Smart Blue.
- Royal Blue, hex #003CC8 (Pantone 2131 C): A rich, saturated medium blue. Used for buttons, interactive elements, link text, and as a gradient endpoint paired with Smart Blue.
- Cyan, hex #00E6FF (Pantone Cyan C): An electric, almost neon cyan. Used for glowing highlight effects on dark backgrounds, data visualization accents, and hover states.

Two additional navy variants are used in dark gradients: Deep Navy #000432 (the darkest value, used at gradient endpoints) and Mid Navy #001080 (used at gradient midpoints).

### Secondary Accent Colors
These are used sparingly — only for category differentiation, status indicators, and visual highlights:
- Purple, hex #644CE6 (Pantone 2725 C): Innovation and AI-related themes.
- Mint Green, hex #02D28C (Pantone 3385 C): Success states, positive metrics, and ESG content.
- Coral, hex #FF6E82 (Pantone 1777 C): Attention moments and humanistic accents.
- Yellow, hex #FFD700 (Pantone Yellow 012 C): Distribution/logistics themes and highlight callouts.

### Neutral Colors
Body text uses #333333 (dark) or #666666 (secondary). Light section backgrounds use #F5F7FA (a soft cool gray). Card borders and dividers use #E2E8F0. Pure white (#FFFFFF) is used for cards, forms, and primary content surfaces.

### Color Ratio
DigiWin's color system follows a 60/30/10 distribution: 60% Smart Blue as the dominant visual presence, 30% from the navy/royal blue/cyan family providing depth and structure, and 10% accent colors (purple, green, coral, yellow) for selective highlights and differentiation.

### Standard Gradients
Hero backgrounds use a 135-degree gradient sweeping from Deep Navy (#000432) through Dark Navy (#000864) to Mid Navy (#001080). Primary action buttons use a 135-degree gradient from Smart Blue (#00AFF0) to Royal Blue (#003CC8). Light-to-white section transitions use a 180-degree gradient from #F5F7FA to #FFFFFF.

## Typography

### Primary Typeface: Noto Sans
All DigiWin text — headings, body copy, UI elements — is set in the Noto Sans family. Headings use weights 500 (Medium) through 700 (Bold) with a line-height of 1.3. Body text uses weight 400 (Regular) at a base size of 16 pixels with a line-height between 1.6 and 1.7. Thai-language text uses Noto Sans Thai. Chinese text uses Noto Sans SC (Simplified Chinese) or Noto Sans TC (Traditional Chinese). The system fallback stack is Arial, then Helvetica.

### Secondary Typeface: JetBrains Mono
JetBrains Mono is used exclusively for labels, tags, badges, and technical identifiers — never for body text or headings. It is always styled uppercase with letter-spacing of approximately 0.1em (1 to 2 pixels), weight 500, and a font size between 11 and 12 pixels. Label color is either #5B6B80 (muted blue-gray for subtle labels) or #0369A1 (deeper blue for section labels). Examples of JetBrains Mono usage: "SINCE 1982", "SET: CCET", "TWSE: 4555", "Month 1", "Recurring Campaign", "ERP", "MES".

### Desktop Type Scale
Hero headlines (H1): clamp between 36px and 56px, weight 700. Section headings (H2): 36 to 40 pixels, weight 700. Card titles (H3): 18 to 22 pixels, weight 600. Body paragraphs: 16 pixels, weight 400. Captions and labels: 11 to 14 pixels.

### Color by Context
Headings on light backgrounds are colored Dark Navy (#000864). Headings and all text on dark backgrounds are white (#FFFFFF). Smart Blue (#00AFF0) is used for emphasized words within hero headlines (for example, "Let's Talk About Your Factory" where "Your Factory" appears in Smart Blue). Body text on light backgrounds is #333333; secondary body text is #666666.

## Graphic System

### The Super D
The D mark from the DigiWin logo is extracted and used independently as a large-scale architectural graphic device called the Super D (超级符号 in Chinese). The Super D appears oversized, cropped at edges, and bleeding off the boundaries of the composition. It is never placed small, centered, or floating. It functions as background architecture — as if the page layout is built around and on top of the D shape.

The Super D has several rendering variants: gradient fill (Smart Blue to Royal Blue), outline only (thin stroke at low opacity between 6% and 10%, used for subtle background texture), particle texture (the D shape filled with a dot-matrix pattern), and 3D render (a dimensional version for premium or event contexts). Common placements are the bottom-right corner, top-right corner, or bleeding from the left or right edge of a section.

### Particle Wave / Data Ocean Current (数据洋流)
DigiWin's signature motion graphic is a three-dimensional perspective dot-grid that undulates like ocean waves. This is not a random scatter of dots. It is dots arranged in precise rows and columns on a surface that is deformed by mathematical wave functions, viewed from a low angle so that foreground dots appear large and opaque while background dots shrink and fade with perspective.

On dark navy backgrounds, the dots glow in Cyan (#00E6FF), creating a vivid "data flow" effect. On light backgrounds, the dots appear in Smart Blue (#00AFF0) at very low opacity (nearly a watermark). A vortex variant shows the dots spiraling into the Super D mark, used on certificates, presentations, and event backdrops. The concept behind this pattern is "data flowing like an ocean current, where each dot represents a data point."

### Grain Texture
Dark gradient sections include a subtle film-grain overlay — an SVG-based noise pattern at 2 to 4 percent opacity. This prevents large navy gradient areas from appearing flat and digitally sterile, adding a tactile, physical quality.

## Animation and Motion

Scroll-triggered fade-in animations use 0.4-second ease timing with a 30-pixel vertical translate, staggered at 0.07-second intervals between sibling elements. Hover transitions on buttons, cards, and links last 0.3 to 0.4 seconds with ease timing. The particle wave drifts horizontally on a continuous 30-second infinite loop. The Super D outline draws itself using a stroke-dasharray animation over 1.2 seconds on page load. Client logo marquees scroll continuously at a 35-second loop speed using seamless element cloning.

All animations respect the prefers-reduced-motion media query. When a user has reduced motion enabled, all animations and transitions are replaced with instant state changes.

## Photography Tiers

DigiWin uses three photography styles:
1. Brand/Abstract: Particle fields, data flow visualizations, and abstract blue light patterns. Used for hero section backgrounds and brand-level moments.
2. Technology: Factory interiors with a cool blue color cast, screens displaying dashboards and analytics, robotic arms and production lines. Used for product and industry pages.
3. Humanistic: Warm-toned portraits of real people in factory or office settings. Used for case studies, team sections, and the about page.

All photography receives a slightly desaturated treatment with a subtle blue tint to maintain visual consistency with the brand palette.

## UI Component Patterns

Cards have a white background, a 1-pixel border in #E2E8F0, border-radius between 12 and 20 pixels, and a box-shadow of 0 4px 24px with rgba(0,0,0,0.06). On hover, the shadow deepens to 0 12px 40px rgba(0,0,0,0.08) and the border color transitions to Smart Blue (#00AFF0).

Primary buttons use the Smart Blue to Royal Blue gradient with white text, border-radius of 12 pixels, and padding of 16 by 32 pixels. Ghost (outline) buttons have a transparent background with a 1-pixel white or navy border. Button labels are action-oriented and welcoming: "Let's Talk," "Get in Touch," "Let's Talk About Your Factory," or "Let's Talk Partnership." DigiWin never uses "Book a Demo" or "Request Demo" because the company does not offer self-service product demos.

Stat counters display a large number in Smart Blue (#00AFF0) set in Noto Sans Bold, with a smaller descriptive label below in muted gray. Section labels use JetBrains Mono uppercase, 11 to 12 pixels, with 2-pixel letter spacing, in color #0369A1.

Dark sections use a navy gradient background layered with grain texture, white text, cyan accents, and a Super D outline at one corner. Light sections use white or #F5F7FA backgrounds with navy text and Smart Blue accents.

## Brand Voice (as expressed through design)

The visual language communicates an identity that is authoritative but welcoming — a trusted, experienced manufacturing technology partner rather than a flashy startup or aggressive software vendor. Every area of white space is purposeful (no wasted areas; content fills meaningfully or the space is tightened). Visual hierarchy is clear and scannable, favoring bullet points and structured layouts over dense paragraphs. The overall aesthetic is "premium industrial": the sophistication and credibility of a global enterprise (44 years, 50,000+ clients, stock-listed) delivered with the warmth and approachability of a local Thai team.
