## Step 3: Factory Path Preview

*3 Understanding Checks — Does the visitor recognize these problems?*

### Understanding Check 1: "Shadow Excel" Reality

> **"You know your factory isn't actually running on your current system—it's running on spreadsheets because your staff finds the software too rigid or slow to handle daily changes."**

**The Problem It Names:**
> Your real operations live in unconnected Excel files, not in your ERP. Financial data doesn't match physical reality because planners work *outside* the system.

**Why this works:**
- Validates the factory owner's "dirty secret" that vendors rarely acknowledge
- Addresses the fear that a new system will also be too rigid
- From *IDP Report* and *The Weight of the Middleman*

---

### Understanding Check 2: "The Black Box" Problem

> **"You know exactly how much raw material you bought and how many finished goods you sold, but the 3 weeks in between (WIP) are invisible."**

**The Problem It Names:**
> You rely on paper reports filled out yesterday to understand what happened today. You can't see what's happening on the floor right now without walking over and asking.

**Why this works:**
- Speaks to the "Control" fear—owners feel blind to their own operations
- Addresses the gap between "Accounting Reality" and "Physical Reality"
- From *Website PRD* and *Presale Transcript*

---

### Understanding Check 3: "Ghost Inventory" Crisis

> **"The system says you have 100 units, but the shelf has 50. This discrepancy forces your team to hoard 'safety stock' just to survive."**

**The Problem It Names:**
> Inventory inaccuracy ties up millions in unnecessary capital and destroys your ability to promise reliable delivery dates. The "borrowing culture" has replaced discipline.

**Why this works:**
- Addresses both financial (capital) and operational (delivery) pain
- Names the human behavior ("borrowing") that technology alone cannot solve
- From *Website PRD* and *IDP Report*

## Step 4: Distributor Path Preview

*3 Understanding Checks — Does the visitor recognize these traps?*

### Understanding Check 1: "The Man-Day Trap"

> **"Your revenue is mathematically capped by your headcount. You cannot grow your top line without proportionally increasing your payroll costs."**

**The Trap It Names:**
> You are selling hours, not assets. Revenue = Hours Worked, making exponential growth impossible. Every January, you start at zero again.

**Why this works:**
- Frames stagnation as a structural economic problem, not a personal failure
- From *IDP Report* and *Partner Website Brief*

---

### Understanding Check 2: "The Customization Death Spiral"

> **"You accept customization requests to win the deal ('sales promise'), but then you become married to that code forever."**

**The Trap It Names:**
> You can't upgrade clients on custom code. Support becomes a nightmare of unbillable hours. Your profit evaporated the moment you said "Yes" to a non-standard request.

**Why this works:**
- Identifies the exact moment margins die
- Validates the frustration of being trapped by past promises
- From *Validation Insights* and *IDP Report*

---

### Understanding Check 3: "The Ghost IT Burden"

> **"You aren't just their ERP consultant—you are their unpaid IT department, fixing Wi-Fi, printers, and user discipline issues because 80% of Thai SMEs lack an internal IT Manager."**

**The Trap It Names:**
> You subsidize their operations with your margins. The "free work" that kills your spirit but that you feel forced to do to keep the relationship.

**Why this works:**
- Acknowledges the invisible, uncompensated labor
- From *IDP Report* and *The Weight of the Middleman*
